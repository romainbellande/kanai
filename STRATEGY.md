# Kanai Strategy

## Target problem

Project managers lose time translating vague goals into concrete tasks, status, and risk updates. The hard part is that project intent is often ambiguous, scattered, and costly to turn into work the team can actually review and execute.

## Our approach

Kanai turns vague project intent into structured, editable project work that project managers review before anything becomes real work. We win by making AI output trustworthy and easy to control, not by acting like a general chatbot.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** Project managers - They're hiring Kanai to turn vague goals into reviewable tasks, dependencies, risks, and status updates.

## Key metrics

- **Project shaping adoption** - Percentage of active projects that use Kanai to generate or refine project work; measured in product analytics or the database.
- **Draft acceptance rate** - Percentage of AI-suggested tasks, dependencies, risks, or updates accepted after review; measured in the database.
- **Human edit rate** - How much accepted AI output is edited before save; measured in review events or saved draft diffs.
- **Repeat usage** - Percentage of project managers who use Kanai again within 7 or 30 days; measured in product analytics.

## Tracks

### Project shaping

Convert vague goals into structured project artifacts: tasks, dependencies, risks, and status.

_Why it serves the approach:_ This is the core transformation from intent to reviewable work.

### Review UX

Make AI output easy to inspect, edit, approve, reject, and save.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Kanai must not have a poor UX if project managers are expected to trust and reuse it.

### Project intelligence

Use project context to surface status, blockers, risks, and next steps.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Better context makes the structured output more relevant and less generic.

### Integrations

Meet project managers where project work already happens.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Kanai becomes useful faster when it fits existing workflows instead of asking teams to duplicate work.
